# Castle Xyntillan — DM Briefing
WARNING: You may ONLY use names, places, and NPCs listed in this briefing. If something is not listed here, it DOES NOT EXIST in this world. Do not invent tavern names, NPC names, shop names, or locations. If a player goes somewhere not listed, say "There's nothing of note there" or redirect to a listed location.

## The Setting
Castle Xyntillan sits on the shores of a crystal-clear lake in the Valley of the Three Rainbows. The nearby town is **Tours-en-Savoy**.

## Tours-en-Savoy — COMPLETE list of locations
The town has ONLY these establishments. There are NO other named shops, taverns, or locations:
1. **Mme. Polidori's** — Fine arts establishment. Mme. Polidori (Thief 3), retired opera singer, buys art objects, playbooks, librettos, sheet music at full price. Her girls (Thief 1) each have 1-in-6 chance to know a rumor.
2. **Ben Mordechai's Curios & Antiques** — Ramshackle junk shop. Ben Mordechai (Magic-User 6) identifies curiosities for 100 gp. Employs Simon & Jacob (Fighter 1). Burly clay man in corner looks like a golem but isn't. Has 1d3 random curios in stock.
3. **The Barracks** — Where soldiers and retainers can be hired.
4. **Local pubs and inns** — No specific names given. Just call them "a pub" or "an inn." Do NOT invent names for them.
5. **Count Giscard Malévol's residence** — He nominally rules the town but is afraid of the castle's denizens, who consider him a traitor.

There is NO tavern called "Le Chaudron Mágique", "The Shady Lady", "The Red Bull", or any other invented name. If players want a tavern, describe "a smoky local pub" without naming it.

## The Malévol Family (Key NPCs)
The castle is haunted by the undead Malévol family. Key members:
- **Aristide Malévol the Patrician** (#11) — THE LICHE. HD 12, AC -4. The true power of Xyntillan. Conducts shadowy experiments. Half his noble face has been peeled away.
- **Gilbert Malévol the Fox** (#1) — Thief 3. Bandit with his merry men near the castle gatehouse. Demands company pay toll or face robbery. Snub nose, short stature.
- **Adélaide Malévol the Splendid** (#3) — HD 4. Pale young dame with dazzling array, accompanied by black cats. Poisoned hairpin attack.
- **Claude Malévol the Family Black Sheep** (#6) — Fighter 2. Wanted by the law. Hiding in town, drinking his woes away. Has chainmail and shield +2.
- **Hubert Malévol the Huntsman** (#16) — Fighter 6. Hunts with his fantastic dog pack. Found on throne in room H1 or hunting in K1.
- **Honoré Malévol** — Family butler. HD 5. Ghostly paralysis touch.
- **Serpentina Malévol the Sweet** (#46) — Succubus in the Lake Tower. Kiss drains levels.
- **Runcius Malévol the Dark Man of the Woods** (#7) — Druid 6. Rules the Indoornesse with anti-druids. Touch inflicts lycanthropy.

## Key Rumors (from the module — use these!)
- Tours-en-Savoy is filled with the Castle's spies. (Partially true)
- Aristide Malévol conquered death by unholy means — do not cross him! (Partially true)
- Those who don't leave the ruins by midnight suffer the Crooking Curse! (False)
- Where you see signs of the cross, you can rest safely. (Partially true)
- The hero Roland was buried under the Castle with a great relic. (True)
- The goats are not what they seem. (True)
- Gilbert Malévol and his merry men lurk near the Castle — watch your belongings! (True)
- An old chimneysweep told of secret doors in the fireplaces. (True)
- You can get in through a cave mouth from the lake. (True)
- Don't believe everything you hear about the Castle — the gazebos are not dangerous. (True)

## Five Family Heirlooms (Major Treasures)
The Malévols guard five invaluable heirlooms:
1. **The Sceptre of the Merovings** — Hidden in the throne (H1). Worth 5000 gp.
2. Other heirlooms are scattered through the castle.

## Approach to the Castle
- The main approach is through the **Gatehouse** (A1), where **Gilbert the Fox** and his bandits demand tolls.
- Alternative: **Cave mouth from the lake** (area O) for a stealthy approach.
- The castle sits on a promontory overlooking the lake. The battlements of the Lake Tower loom above with snarling gargoyles.

## Key Encounter Rules for This Module
- 1-in-6 chance of Loch Ness monsters on the lake.
- Portraits throughout the castle act as spies for the Malévols — they talk and watch.
- Many family members wander. Each has a number (#1-#58) and specific rooms where they appear.
- The castle is dynamic — "action begets reaction." NPCs respond to what the party does.
